Privacy, plainly

  • What's stored: the cards, reward categories, credits, offers, receipts, and balances you track — locally on your device only.
  • How data gets in: you enter it, scan a receipt with the camera, or let the optional Safari extension read a balance off a page you're already signed in to. Nothing is fetched behind your back.
  • Bank credentials: never requested, never stored. CardPecker doesn't log in to your accounts — it doesn't even need your card numbers. The sync extension only reads what's already shown on your screen, and never your passwords.
  • Location: used only while you're in the app, to surface nearby card-linked offers — never stored, never shared. It's entirely optional.
  • Backups: optional, and they go to your own iCloud Drive — there is no CardPecker server.
  • What CardPecker doesn't do: no account, no analytics, no advertising identifiers, no data sold or shared.

The full policy is short and readable: CardPecker Privacy Policy.

Frequently asked questions

Does CardPecker connect to my bank?

No. CardPecker never asks for bank credentials and never logs in to your accounts. You add your cards and their reward categories manually — no card numbers needed. If you want balances filled in automatically, the optional Safari extension reads only what's already displayed on a page you signed in to yourself.

How does it know which card earns the most?

You record each card's earning rates by category. When you tap a category, CardPecker compares the effective return across your cards and shows the winner — including subcategory rules like specific airlines or hotel brands.

How do credits and offers work?

The Coupon Book tracks statement credits and recurring benefits and auto-refreshes them each billing period, so expiring credits surface before the clock runs out. Pair it with our benefit-tracking guide.

Does anything sync or leave my device?

CardPecker is built local-first: your data is stored on your device, with no account and no tracking. Two things are optional and under your control — backups to your own iCloud Drive, and the Safari sync extension, which hands balances and offers from your screen to the app on the same device. See the privacy policy for the details.

What is CardPecker Rewards?

An optional one-time purchase that unlocks the rewards-balance tracker: keep miles and points for programs like Chase, Amex, Hyatt, and Delta in one place, see their cash value with up-to-date point valuations, and view your whole portfolio at a glance. The core which-card recommendations stay free.

Is CardPecker financial advice?

No. CardPecker is an organizational tool — it compares the reward rules you enter. It doesn't recommend cards, and nothing on this page is financial, legal, or tax advice.
